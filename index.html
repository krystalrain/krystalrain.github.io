About Me

Alex is an M.S. Information Security Policy & Management student at Heinz College. She has an academic passion for ethics, data scince, politcal philosophy, and cybersecurrity. During summer 2021, she is working with the Center for Security and Emerging Technology as part of their CyberAI project, investigating the ways artificial intelligence will impact future cyberspace safety and policy. Perviously she worked with the Census Bureau’s xD team on combating data bias in federal government data science work, and as a volunteer with Data for Black Lives. Alex is currently involved in research on the philosophical and pedagogical approaches to teaching ethics in computing. In her spare time, she’s an active member in effective altruism spaces concerned with transformative and misalignment risks in AI. She graduated from Carnegie Mellon University in 2020 with a degree in Ethics History and Public Policy and Minor in Physics.
